Beyond the core scientific process, these general tools and tips can significantly enhance your research experience.

  • Citation Management & Academic Writing:
    • Zotero (Free, open-source reference management)
    • Mendeley (Reference manager and academic social network)
    • Grammarly (Writing assistant)
    • Scribbr: Citation Guides (APA, MLA, Chicago)
    • BibMe (Citation generator)
  • Note-Taking, Organization & Collaboration:
    • Notion (All-in-one workspace for notes, tasks, databases)
    • Evernote (Note-taking and organization)
    • Google Keep (Simple note-taking)
    • Google Docs/Sheets/Slides (Collaboration suite)
    • Trello (Visual project management)
    • Asana (Work management platform)
  • Science Communication & Critical Thinking:
    • SciComm.xyz (Resources for science communication)
    • Coursera: Critical Thinking Courses (Online courses)
    • edX: Scientific Thinking Courses (Online courses)
    • TED Talks: The Scientific Method & Critical Thinking
  • General Productivity & Learning:
    • Forest (Focus timer app)
    • Pomodoro Technique (Time management method)
    • MindMeister (Online mind mapping)
